I am a Ph.D. researcher under the guidance of the esteemed Dr. Romila Pradhan at the Department of Computer and Information Technology, Purdue University, specializing in Responsible Data Science, Machine Learning Trustworthiness, Reinforcement Learning, and Data Preparation. My research focuses on developing rigorous data-driven methodologies to ensure fairness and transparency in machine learning models. With a strong mathematical foundation, I strive to bridge the gap between theoretical principles and real-world applications in data-driven decision-making.

My work primarily involves data acquisition, data enrichment, and bias mitigation to enhance the reliability of machine learning algorithms. I develop methodologies to identify, quantify, and mitigate biases in datasets, ensuring equitable outcomes in AI models. Additionally, I explore efficient data acquisition strategies, leveraging reinforcement learning and statistical and optimization techniques to refine training data and improve model generalization.

I bring a rigorous analytical approach to solving complex problems in data science. My expertise spans probability theory, optimization, and statistical modeling, which form the backbone of my research in trustworthy and explainable AI. Through my work, I aim to contribute to the broader mission of ethical AI, ensuring that machine learning systems operate with fairness, accountability, and transparency.
